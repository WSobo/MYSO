INTRO

A key component of all science and engineering disciplines is the ability to obtain reliable and repeatable data [iGEM link]. This year's Interlab Measurement Study is intended to establish a baseline of lab-to-lab reproducibility of multi-color fluorescence development over time. In addition to the calibration experiment, our team participated in two out of the three Interlab Study experiments. The protocols for these experiments, as well as the measurement calibration kit and InterLab devices, were all provided in the 2022 iGEM distribution. All teams participating in the 2022 iGEM Interlab study were required to use the provided materials and transform into the same strain of E. coli (DH5α). This minimizes the amount of variables between labs, making the only significant difference the 96-well plate reader that is used for fluorescent measurements, as there are many different makes and models that teams may use.

Calibration Experiment

Summary: The three-color calibration protocol was completed prior to beginning any of the experiments. It includes making stocks and working solutions, making and measuring serial dilutions of each of the three calibrant dyes and the particle calibrant. The main purpose of the Calibration Experiment was to properly calibrate the optical density reader for Interlab future experiments [1].

  • Calibration Protocol
  • Results


Experiment 1

Summary: The purpose of this experiment is to assess the lab-to-lab reproducibility of the new three color calibration protocol provided by iGEM [1].
In this experiment, we measured the fluorescence of six devices, or plasmids, that encoded either a single fluorescence protein (blue, green, or red) or two fluorescence proteins encoded in two transcriptional units. The fluorescence of these devices were calibrated to the three calibrant dyes and the optical density of the culture was calibrated to the cell density calibrant. [iGEM citation at the bottom of the page]

Experiment 2

This experiment aims to assess the lab-to-lab reproducibility of the three color calibration protocol when two fluorescent proteins are expressed in the same cell. [iGEM citation at the bottom of the page]
In this experiment, we measured the fluorescence of six devices that encode two fluorescence proteins in two transcriptional units. The devices differ in the order of the transcriptional units. We calibrated the fluorescence of these devices to the calibrant dyes and the optical density of the culture to the cell density calibrant. [iGEM citation at the bottom of the page]
